Motivation

London Fire Brigade is London's fire and rescue service and one of the largest
firefighting and rescue organisations in the world. Being that, they are
a very important organisation and are always very, very busy in aiding Londoners.
What's more is that information on all incidents they attend to is readily available
for whoever wants to investigate it. That's exactly what this project is about.
We are going to investigate the LFB incidents and give a broad view over the organisation's
work. Additionally, we are going to investigate whether there is some correlation between
these incidents and other demographic information.

Project overview

Datasets

Below, each dataset used in this project is described.
Links to the datasets are also provided.

  • London Fire Brigade Incident Records

    This dataset contains the details of every incident responded by London Fire Brigade. It contains information about when and where the incident happened and the type of incident dealt with. The records are from January of 2009 to February of 2021. The dataset was obtained from here.

  • London Fire Brigade Mobilisation Records

    This dataset contains the details of every fire engine sent to an incident's scene. It contains information about the appliance used, where it was deployed from and response times. The records are from January of 2009 to February of 2021. The dataset was obtained from here.

  • Housing in London

    This dataset contains information about houses in London, all the way from January of 1995 to January of 2021. Average price by area, houses sold and population size are among the details provided. The dataset was obtained from here.

  • House ages in London

    This dataset contains the building periods of houses in London. The dataset was obtained from here.

  • London Borough Profiles

    This dataset paints a general picture of the boroughs of London by presenting a range of topics covering demographic, economic, social and environmental statistics for each borough. The data ranges from 2011 to 2017. The dataset was obtained from here.

  • UK postcodes

    This dataset contains all levels of postcodes in the United Kingdom. It is used to make a connection between other datasets which don't use the same postcode information in London. The dataset was obtained from here.

  • Boundary Files for London

    This dataset contains shapefiles for London. It is used to draw London and it's borough borders for certain maps. The dataset was obtained from here.

Housing prices and incidents

Quite interistingly, the average price for a house is well correlated with the reported amount of fires. This makes sense, as newer more expensive buildings tend to better prepared for these types of incidents. On the other hand however, these more expensive houses also report false alarms far more often. It therefore seems that cheaper areas care less about AFA systems. All things considered, it seems that expensive neighboorhoods are generally safer than cheaper ones, at least in terms of fire incidents.

Location specific incident predictions

Some incidents are more likely to occur than others in certain areas.

Based on the collected geographical data and machine learning tools,
it is possible to predict the most likely incident type to occur at any specific location.

The maps below all show the most likely incident type to occur at specific longitude and lattitude values

Incident groups

It is clear that both fires, special services and false alarms are reported all around London.

Fires do however seem to occur more often in the north-western and south-western outskirts of London. There areas tend to be less populated and contain more green areas.

Property categories

As for property categories, boat related incidents tend to follow the rivers and lakes surrounding London nicely. Aircraft related incidents also surrounds Londons largest airports nicely. For all other places, road incidents non-surprisingly occur far more often.

False alarms:
Malicius or good intend?

Luckily, false alarms tend to be based on good intend far more often than not. There does however seem to be a slight tendency of malicius reports around Loughton.

Special Service Type

The dataset holds information regarding a wide amount of special service types. By investigating RTC (Road traffic collision), Lift releases, Suicide attempts and flooding, we get this widely coloured graph. RTC incidents clearly occurs much more frequently in the outskirts of the city around highways, whereas lift releases are more centered around areas with more dense populations. Flooding seems to be a problem in everywhere, as does suicides.

Note that all samples are balanced in this Visualization, meaning that the proportions between each type might differ in reality. For unbalanced Visualizations, see the explainer notebook.
